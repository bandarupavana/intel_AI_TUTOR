Advanced Vocabulary – Word Choice & Collocations

📘 Lesson Overview:

At an advanced level, vocabulary learning isn't just about knowing difficult words, but understanding their subtle meanings, how they’re used in context, and which words naturally go together (collocations).

✨ Examples of Word Choice (Synonyms with Different Tones):

  • Say vs. Declare vs. Announce – “declare” is more formal or official.
  • Big vs. Substantial vs. Massive – “massive” suggests scale and sometimes heaviness.
  • Help vs. Assist vs. Facilitate – “facilitate” often implies making a process easier.

📌 Common Collocations:

  • Make an effort
  • Catch someone’s attention
  • Strong coffee / Heavy rain
  • Break the news
  • Deeply concerned

🔍 Practice Questions:

  1. Choose the best word: "The manager will _____ the new policy tomorrow." (say / announce / talk)
  2. Find the odd collocation: "Make progress, Do a decision, Take a risk"
  3. Use “substantial” in your own sentence correctly.
  4. What’s the difference between “argue” and “debate”?
  5. Create a sentence using the collocation “catch someone’s attention.”

✍️ Task:

Write a short description of a news event. Use at least five advanced vocabulary words and three strong collocations from the lesson above.
