1. Kinds of Sentences

Sentences can have different meanings. Look at these examples:

  • Statement: Tells something. (e.g., The sun is bright.)
  • Question: Asks something. (e.g., Where is my teddy?)
  • Command: Tells someone what to do. (e.g., Please sit down.)
  • Exclamation: Shows excitement. (e.g., Wow! This cake is yummy!)

Try this: Change the sentences below:

  • The dog is running. (Make it a question)
  • Look at the rainbow! (Make it a statement)

2. Matching Words (Subjects & Verbs)

Words must match in a sentence. Look at these:

  • One cat runs.
  • Two cats run.

Practice: Choose the right word:

  • The baby (cry/cries) at night.
  • My friends (play/plays) with me.

3. Talking About Time (Tenses)

When did something happen? Look:

  • Past: Yesterday, I played.
  • Present: Now, I play.
  • Future: Tomorrow, I will play.

Practice: Change these:

  • I eat an apple. (Change to past)
  • She will jump. (Change to present)

4. Words in a Sentence (Parts of Speech)

Every word has a job. Look:

  • Noun: Name of a person, place, or thing. (e.g., dog, park)
  • Verb: An action word. (e.g., run, jump)
  • Adjective: Describes something. (e.g., big, red)
  • Adverb: Tells how something happens. (e.g., slowly, quickly)

Find the words: Identify the noun and verb:

  • The bird sings a song.
  • Tom rides a bike.
