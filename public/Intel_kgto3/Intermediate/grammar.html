1. Types of Sentences

We use different kinds of sentences when we talk and write:

  • Statement: Tells something. (e.g., I have a pet dog.)
  • Question: Asks something. (e.g., Where is my toy?)
  • Command: Tells someone what to do. (e.g., Please sit down.)
  • Exclamation: Shows strong feelings. (e.g., Wow! Thatâ€™s amazing!)

2. Singular and Plural

When we talk about one thing, we use **singular** words. When we talk about more than one, we use **plural** words:

  • Singular: One apple.
  • Plural: Two apples.
  • Singular: A cat sleeps.
  • Plural: Cats sleep.

3. Past, Present, and Future

Words can tell us when something happens:

  • Past: I **played** with my toys.
  • Present: I **play** with my toys.
  • Future: I **will play** with my toys.
