1. Types of Sentences

There are four types of sentences, each serving a unique purpose:

  • Declarative: States a fact or opinion. (e.g., The Amazon rainforest is home to diverse wildlife.)
  • Interrogative: Asks a question. (e.g., Why do birds migrate in winter?)
  • Imperative: Gives a command or request. (e.g., Please complete your homework on time.)
  • Exclamatory: Expresses strong emotion. (e.g., Wow! That roller coaster was thrilling!)

Practice: Convert the following sentences into different types:

  • She loves reading mystery novels. (Change to an interrogative sentence)
  • Close the window before it rains. (Change to an exclamatory sentence)

2. Subject-Verb Agreement

The subject and verb must always match in number:

  • Singular: The cat sleeps on the sofa.
  • Plural: The cats sleep on the sofa.
  • Incorrect: The cat sleep on the sofa.

Special Cases:

  • Indefinite Pronouns: Words like "everyone" and "nobody" are singular. (e.g., Everyone enjoys the holiday.)
  • Compound Subjects: When joined by "and," the subject is plural. (e.g., Alex and Mia love hiking.)
  • Either/Neither: If followed by "or" or "nor," the verb agrees with the closest subject. (e.g., Neither the teacher nor the students are absent.)

Exercise: Choose the correct verb:

  • The basket of apples (is/are) on the table.
  • Either my mom or my dad (drives/drive) me to school.

3. Tenses and Their Usage

There are three main tenses, each with different forms:

  • Past: Actions that happened before now. (e.g., She visited the museum last week.)
  • Present: Actions happening now. (e.g., She visits the museum every Saturday.)
  • Future: Actions that will happen. (e.g., She will visit the museum next week.)

Perfect Tenses:

  • Past Perfect: She had finished her work before dinner.
  • Present Perfect: He has traveled to several countries.
  • Future Perfect: By tomorrow, they will have completed the project.

Exercise: Rewrite the sentence in different tenses:

  • She studies for her exams. (Change to past perfect)
  • They will go to the beach. (Change to present perfect)

4. Parts of Speech

Every word in a sentence belongs to one of the eight parts of speech:

  • Noun: Names a person, place, or thing. (e.g., Sarah, school, book)
  • Pronoun: Replaces a noun. (e.g., he, she, they)
  • Verb: Shows action or state of being. (e.g., run, jump, is)
  • Adjective: Describes a noun. (e.g., beautiful, strong)
  • Adverb: Describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb. (e.g., quickly, silently)
  • Preposition: Shows the relationship between a noun and another word. (e.g., under, between)
  • Conjunction: Connects words or clauses. (e.g., and, but)
  • Interjection: Expresses emotion. (e.g., Wow! Oops!)

Challenge: Identify the parts of speech in the sentence:

  • The small dog ran quickly across the street.
  • Wow! That was an incredible performance.
